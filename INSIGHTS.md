# 📊 Key Insights — Olist E-Commerce Customer Behavior & Sales Intelligence

---

## 1. 📈 Revenue Growth & Trend

- Olist experienced **rapid revenue growth** from near-zero in late 2016 to a peak of **~R$1.2 million** in November 2017 (likely driven by Black Friday / holiday season).
- Revenue stabilised in a **~R$1.0–1.2M band** throughout early-to-mid 2018, indicating a maturing platform.
- A **sharp drop** occurs in late September / October 2018 — this is almost certainly a **data truncation artefact** rather than a real business collapse, as the dataset's collection window closes around that time.
- **Seasonality signal:** November consistently outperforms surrounding months, pointing to strong holiday-season demand.

---

## 2. 👥 Customer Segmentation (RFM Analysis)

| Segment | Count | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Potential Loyalists | ~41,000 | Nurture with personalised offers |
| Loyal Customers | ~34,000 | Reward & retain |
| At Risk | ~13,000 | Re-engagement campaigns |
| Champions | ~8,000 | VIP treatment, referral programmes |
| Lost Customers | ~3,000 | Win-back or deprioritise |

- The majority of the customer base (~75%) falls in the **Potential Loyalists + Loyal Customers** buckets — a healthy sign of repeat-purchase behaviour.
- Only **~3%** are classified as Champions, revealing significant headroom to convert Loyal Customers into top-tier buyers.
- The **At Risk + Lost** cohort (~16%) represents a churn risk worth targeting with re-engagement.

---

## 3. ⭐ Customer Satisfaction (Review Scores)

- **57,000+ orders** received a perfect score of **5/5** — the single largest group.
- 4-star reviews account for ~19,000 orders, meaning **roughly 77%+ of reviews are positive (4–5 stars)**.
- 1-star reviews (~11,500) significantly outnumber 2-star (~3,000) and 3-star (~8,000), a classic **J-curve** pattern where dissatisfied customers are more motivated to leave a review than neutral ones.
- **Recommendation:** Investigate root causes of 1-star reviews (likely delivery delays or damaged goods) to improve NPS.

---

## 4. 💳 Payment Preferences

- **Credit card** is the dominant payment method (~77,000 transactions), reflecting Brazilian consumers' preference for instalment-based purchases (parcelamento).
- **Boleto** (Brazil's bank slip) is a distant second (~20,000), widely used by unbanked or credit-averse customers.
- **Vouchers** (~6,000) and **debit cards** (~1,500) play minor roles.
- **Opportunity:** Expanding BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later) or increasing credit-card instalment options could further drive conversion.

---

## 5. 💰 Price Distribution

- Prices are **heavily right-skewed** — the vast majority of products are priced **below R$200**, with a long tail extending to R$7,000+.
- The highest-frequency bin is **R$0–100**, accounting for the bulk (~84,000+) of all items sold.
- High-value outliers (R$1,000+) exist but are rare, suggesting Olist primarily serves the **mass-market / affordable segment**.
- **Implication:** Marketing and logistics optimisation should be tuned for low-cost, high-volume SKUs.

## 6. 🗺️ Geographic Distribution (Top States)

| Rank | State | Orders |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | SP (São Paulo) | ~41,500 |
| 2 | RJ (Rio de Janeiro) | ~12,800 |
| 3 | MG (Minas Gerais) | ~11,600 |
| 4 | RS (Rio Grande do Sul) | ~5,400 |
| 5 | PR (Paraná) | ~5,100 |

- **São Paulo dominates** with 3× the orders of the next state — consistent with its status as Brazil's largest economy and population centre.
- The **Southeast triangle** (SP + RJ + MG) accounts for the lion's share of demand.
- States like **DF, ES, GO** present **growth opportunities** — relatively low penetration despite decent populations.

---

## 7. 📦 Order Status

- **~97%** of all orders are marked `delivered` — an exceptionally high fulfilment rate.
- `shipped` orders (~1,100) are likely in-transit at the time of data capture.
- `canceled` (~625) and `unavailable` (~600) are minimal, indicating a **reliable supply chain**.
- Very few orders remain in `invoiced`, `processing`, `created`, or `approved` states, suggesting efficient order-processing pipelines.

---

## 🔑 Top Actionable Recommendations

1. **Double down on November** — run targeted promotions ahead of the holiday spike to maximise the seasonal revenue bump.
2. **Convert Potential Loyalists** — they are the largest segment; loyalty-points schemes or subscription perks could push them into the Champions tier.
3. **Reduce 1-star reviews** — audit logistics partners in SP/RJ/MG (highest volume = highest complaint exposure) and improve post-purchase communication.
4. **Expand payment flexibility** — offer more instalment plans and Pix (Brazil's instant payment system) to capture the boleto audience digitally.
5. **Target Southeast growth corridors** — DF, GO, and ES show lower-than-expected penetration for their economic size; geo-specific campaigns could unlock volume.
